  Solutions

      The diagram above outlines a complex long-term social process that average people don't see. So they will continue to try solving surface problems (red box above) without affecting the primary problems causing them.

      Options for breaking this cycle include enforceable federal and state laws requiring that...

  • funding be provided to test and verify or disprove this proposed toxic cycle;

  • all high school, college, and military text books and curricula must explain the cycle objectively, and school and teaching certification must require demonstrated staff knowledge of it,

  • human-service licensing must include testing for comprehension of the cycle and its main impacts on people and families;

  • immigrants must demonstrate comprehension of the cycle and its effects before citizenship is granted;

  • marriage licensing should include mandatory education on the cycle, and screening for psychological wounds

  • public and private health clinics should include programs to help people understand, assess for, and reduce psychological wounds - specially if they're parents

  • jail inmates should receive mandatory education on the cycle and its effects, and optional counseling toward reducing their psychological wounds;

  • doctors and clergy should inform pregnant couples of this toxic cycle and what it can mean to them and any descendents;

  • the media should be encouraged or required to weave knowledge of this cycle into their programming, including public service announcements;

  • welfare programs should inform their clients of the cycle and its effects and provide optional screening and counseling for psychological wounds.

      Laws like these won't happen unless legislators (a) understand and accept the cycle and its effects, or (b) voters demand that federal and state governments enact them. Because of epidemic wounds + denial + unawareness, the latter us unlikely.
